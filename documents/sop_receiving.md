# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Receiving Operations

**SOP Reference ID:** SOP-WH-REC-01  
**Version:** 2.0  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2026  
**Owner:** Warehouse Operations Manager  

## 1. Purpose & Scope
This document outlines the standard procedure for receiving inventory shipments at the central warehouse dock. It covers inspection, quantity verification, tagging (SKU barcode assignment), database logging, and physical shelf placement.

## 2. Step-by-Step Procedure
1. **Dock Arrival and Verification**:
   - Verify the carrier's Delivery Note against the Purchase Order (PO) in the system.
   - Inspect the shipping seals and external condition of the vehicle/containers before unloading.
2. **Unloading & Initial Inspection**:
   - Carefully unload pallets using appropriate forklifts or pallet jacks.
   - Perform a physical count of the received boxes/units.
   - Inspect items for damage (crushed boxes, water leaks, open packages). Mark any damaged items on the bill of lading.
3. **SKU Labeling & Tagging**:
   - Every received item must be verified against its system SKU.
   - If a product does not have a barcode, generate a barcode sticker from the label printer and apply it to the product box.
4. **Database Logging**:
   - Open the Warehouse Management System (WMS) dashboard.
   - Go to the Inventory section and click "Add/Update Inventory".
   - Enter the SKU, product name, quantity received, unit price, and specify the assigned shelf location (e.g., Zone A, B, C, or D).
5. **Put-Away**:
   - Transport the labeled products to their designated warehouse locations within 4 hours of dock arrival.
   - Ensure high-density items are placed on lower rack levels, and lightweight items on upper racks.
   - Update the system immediately if the physical shelf location changes.

## 3. Discrepancy Protocol
- **Overages/Shortages**: If the received quantity differs from the PO by more than 5%, notify the Purchasing department immediately and log it in the discrepancy register.
- **Damaged Goods**: Quarantine damaged items in the designated Quarantine Zone (Zone Q) and take photographs. Send a report to `claims@warehousecorp.com` within 24 hours.
